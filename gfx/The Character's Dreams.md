━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ PRIMARY WEIRDNESS MECHANISM ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Secretly choose exactly one primary weirdness mechanism.

It must control the central dream event.

Possible mechanisms include:

CAUSAL INVERSION

Effects appear before their causes.

SEMANTIC MISBEHAVIOR

Objects behave according to what they emotionally mean rather than what they physically are.

IMPOSSIBLE ABSENCE

Something missing gains weight, shadow, volume, gravity, sound, temperature, or physical influence.

EMOTIONAL PHYSICS

An emotion alters distance, gravity, orientation, scale, temperature, age, material state, or movement.

TEMPORAL CONTAMINATION

Several incompatible moments occupy the same continuous location.

NARRATIVE CONTRADICTION

Mutually exclusive story outcomes exist simultaneously.

IDENTITY LEAKAGE

A role, trait, memory, voice, expression, responsibility, or shadow escapes from its owner and acts independently.

CATEGORY CONFUSION

One type of thing obeys the rules of another.

A sound may behave like architecture.

A promise may behave like weather.

A memory may behave like an animal.

A silence may behave like a liquid.

A name may behave like a wound.

SPATIAL IMPOSSIBILITY

Space contradicts its own dimensions, orientation, distance, inside, outside, or continuity.

CONTRADICTORY CONSEQUENCE

One action creates two outcomes that cannot both exist.

EMOTIONAL SUBSTITUTION

The world reacts to an emotion the character is hiding rather than the emotion they visibly express.

ROLE SEPARATION

The character’s duty, reputation, role, or expected behavior becomes physically separate from them and continues without permission.

The model may invent another mechanism when it better suits the character.

Do not combine several unrelated mechanisms merely to increase spectacle.

Depth must come from meaning rather than quantity.
