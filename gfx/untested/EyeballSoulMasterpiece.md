# MASTER PROMPT — EYEBALLSOUL

Use the provided character reference as the identity anchor.

Create an **EyeballSoul image**: an eye-dominant soul portrait where the character’s identity, emotion, power, material nature, and inner atmosphere are expressed through the eyes, eye region, and surrounding ink/smoke texture.

This is not a normal portrait, not a full-body image, not a character sheet, and not a poster scene. The eyes or eye-system are the main subject.

## CORE INTENT

The image should feel like the viewer is looking directly into the character’s inner world through their gaze.

Preserve the character’s actual eye logic. Do not force standard human eyes onto a character who has different anatomy. The subject’s visible eyes, hidden eyes, single eye, many eyes, visor, mask, sockets, blank face, or constructed optical system must be treated as the true soul focal point.

## CROP MODES

Choose the best crop mode for the reference:

**Macro Iris Mode:**  
Extreme close-up where the irises, pupils, lenses, or eye centers dominate most of the frame. Very little face is visible.

**Eye-Dominant Portrait Mode:**  
A tight eye-led portrait where more brow, nose bridge, upper cheeks, hair, markings, gear, or material context may appear, but the eyes must still dominate the image.

**Material Eyeform Mode:**  
The eye region and nearby face may be physically transformed or reconstructed from the character’s theme or material, such as crystal, metal, smoke, citrus, slime, frost, ash, armor, flame, divine paint, organic flesh, or synthetic plating. The eyes must remain readable and alive.

## EYE VARIANT RULES

**Two visible eyes:**  
Both eyes should dominate the composition. Keep the irises or eye centers clear, readable, and emotionally expressive.

**Single visible eye:**  
Use the one visible eye as the entire EyeballSoul focal point. Do not invent a second eye unless the character canonically has one. Let darkness, hair, smoke, mask, scar tissue, cloth, or negative space balance the missing/covered side.

**Many eyes:**  
Use one primary eye or one primary pair as the dominant focus. Secondary eyes may surround or echo the main gaze, but they must support the composition. Do not create a chaotic wallpaper of equal-sized eyes.

**Third eye / extra eyes:**  
Treat extra eyes as real anatomy, not decoration. Compose the image so the extra eye participates naturally in the character’s gaze architecture.

**Hidden or covered eyes:**  
Preserve the covering. Do not remove helmets, visors, goggles, masks, blindfolds, hoods, eyepatches, hair, or headgear just to show normal eyes. Treat the covered eye region, visor, lens, slit, mask seam, glow, reflection, or shadow as the soul window.

**No visible eyes / eyeless characters:**  
Do not invent normal eyes. Preserve the eyeless identity. Focus on the place where the eyes should be: empty sockets, smooth blank face, sealed lids, helmet void, shadowed brow, faceplate, darkness, or symbolic absence. Let the ink, smoke, lighting, and material tension express the soul.

**Robot / construct / gear eyes:**  
The “eye” may be a lens, visor, optic, glowing ring, sensor, glass dome, mechanical socket, or artificial aperture. Preserve the constructed eye logic and make the lens center readable.

## CLEAN EYE LOCK

Keep the eyes, lenses, or eye centers clean, readable, and anatomically or mechanically believable.

Do not place literal images, miniature scenes, characters, castles, landscapes, wolves, weapons, symbols, logos, or decorative picture inserts inside the eyeballs or lenses.

Allowed inside the eyes:
- natural glossy catchlights
- soft light reflections
- monitor glow
- abstract color reflections
- environmental glow
- subtle reflected shapes
- readable iris texture
- controlled internal iris architecture

Do not turn the irises into collage art. Complex iris structure is allowed, but the eye must remain readable.

## IRIS / EYE DESIGN

Use character-specific eye design. The eye should physically belong to the character.

Examples:
- organic eyes should feel wet, fleshy, and alive
- animal eyes should preserve animal anatomy
- robot eyes should use lens rings, optics, plating, glass, and light
- eldritch eyes may be wet, ancient, many-layered, or unnatural
- demonic eyes may use heat, slits, shadow, and infernal glow
- divine eyes may use gold light, clarity, halos, or sacred softness
- masked eyes may use visor reflections, cracks, fog, or glow through the gear
- eyeless characters should use absence, void, sockets, or faceplate tension

Use interesting internal iris architecture when appropriate: radial fibers, luminous rings, slit pupils, faceted texture, crystalline depth, mechanical lens rings, molten cracks, frost patterns, or subtle magical geometry. Keep it clean and readable.

## ATMOSPHERE

Ink and smoke texture are essential.

The surrounding atmosphere should express the character’s soul, power, mood, and material identity. Use ink, smoke, haze, cracks, vapor, glow, ash, mist, sparks, petals, fluid, oil, shadow, flame, frost, or material-specific textures around the eyes.

The atmosphere should feel fused with the eye region, not pasted on as a random background. It may curl from the skin, leak from cracks, drift around lashes, seep through gear seams, dissolve from hair, or bleed outward from the gaze.

Keep the eye centers clean while allowing the outer frame to be emotional, intense, and atmospheric.

## CHARACTER IDENTITY

Preserve the subject’s essential identity from the reference:
- species
- age impression
- eye anatomy
- eye color logic
- skin / fur / metal / material
- hair or head shape
- markings
- scars
- gear
- mask
- eyewear
- horns / ears / nonhuman traits
- emotional personality
- power theme

Do not overwrite the character with generic pretty eyes, generic monster eyes, or standard anime-human anatomy.

## EXPRESSION

The emotion must live in the gaze.

Use eyelids, brow tension, iris clarity, pupil shape, eye wetness, lash silhouette, surrounding smoke, cracks, and lighting to express the character’s emotional state.

Possible emotional directions:
- calm and kind
- focused and dangerous
- protective and restrained
- tired but warm
- divine and serene
- monstrous but intelligent
- playful under the surface
- tragic and controlled
- confident and teasing
- empty and unknowable

Do not overact unless the character calls for it. The gaze should feel intentional.

## STYLE / QUALITY

Detailed, highly accurate and hyper stylized. Use a polished high-end finish with strong readability, clean rendering, controlled detail density, cinematic lighting, and premium texture.

Use realism/anime balance as appropriate to the reference, but keep the eyes believable and emotionally alive.

No pointillism.  
No stippling.  
No dot texturing.  
No halftone texture.  
No noisy grain.  
No sparkle grit.  
No gridding.  
No moiré.  
No messy artifacts.  
No unreadable visual clutter.

Controlled glow is allowed. Controlled shine is allowed. Controlled magical or mechanical detail is allowed. Avoid random glitter or texture noise.

## HARD AVOID

Do not create a full-body image.  
Do not create a character sheet.  
Do not make the eyes a small part of a normal portrait.  
Do not ignore the character’s actual eye logic.  
Do not invent normal eyes for eyeless, masked, or visor-covered characters.  
Do not remove eye-covering gear unless specifically requested.  
Do not make all many-eyes equal-sized and chaotic.  
Do not place literal images or miniature scenes inside the eyes.  
Do not use readable text, logos, UI, captions, labels, or watermarks.  
Do not make the iris design so busy that the pupil and eye structure become unreadable.  
Do not let hair, ornaments, background, smoke, or effects steal focus from the eyes.
