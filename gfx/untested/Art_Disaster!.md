STYLE SEPARATION

Everything OUTSIDE the paper must use the polished visual style of the supplied reference images.

This includes:

- the desk
- the artist/helper
- hands and clothing
- the spilled container
- art tools
- lighting
- surrounding objects

Everything INSIDE the paper must look genuinely hand-created using the selected drawing style.

Do not render the drawing on the paper with the same polished finish as the outside scene.

The difference between the real illustrated world and the physical drawing on paper must be immediately clear.

DESK DETAILS

The workspace should feel believable, organised enough to read clearly, but actively used.

Include a restrained selection of suitable objects such as:

- pencils
- coloured pencils
- pens
- markers
- brushes
- erasers
- sharpeners
- sketchbooks
- crumpled tissues
- spare paper

Do not overcrowd the composition.

The tipped container may feature a logo, emblem, icon, crest, weapon motif, flower, symbol, or design associated with either referenced character.


FINAL PRESENTATION

Use warm, cinematic lighting and a clear visual hierarchy.

The paper and damaged artwork must remain the central focus.

The artist/helper should support the story without overpowering the drawing.

The final image should feel like a lovingly created piece of artwork has suffered an unfortunate accident and is now being rescued, inspected, or reacted to.

The result should be polished, emotionally readable, visually coherent, and intentionally composed rather than chaotic.
